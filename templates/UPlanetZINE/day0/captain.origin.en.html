Technical prerequisites

Administering an Astroport.ONE station requires skills in:

  • BASH: scripts, variables, pipes, cron, sed/awk
  • Docker: containers, compose, volumes, networks
  • Python venv: virtual environments (~/.astro/)
  • Linux: systemd, journalctl, SSH, permissions
  • Networking: ports, DNS, SSL certificates, IPFS

Assess yourself before proceeding.

Recommended training

If you are not comfortable, start with:

  • BASH: pad.p2p.legal/BASH
  • SHELL: pad.p2p.legal/s/SHELL
  • Docker: pad.p2p.legal/Docker

Astroport.ONE code is 95% bash. Understanding my.sh, environment variables, and the 20h12.process.sh cycle is essential.

Source: github.com/papiche/Astroport.ONE/CLAUDE.md

ORIGIN vs ZEN

  • ORIGIN: open sandbox. swarm.key = 0000. 1 Zen = 0.1 G1. No private swarm.
  • ZEN: production. unique swarm.key. 1 Zen = 1 EUR. Full cooperative services.

To switch to ZEN mode:

  • Generate a unique swarm.key
  • Distribute it to your stations
  • ORIGIN accounts remain (automatic migration)
