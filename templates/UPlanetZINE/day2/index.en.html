Day 2: The Digital Garden

Do you know what a CSA (Community Supported Agriculture) is? A group of citizens funds a farmer, shares the harvest, and co-owns the garden together.

G1FabLab works the same way. But for the Internet. A digital CSA where each member co-owns the infrastructure instead of renting from Google.

Traditional CSA / Digital CSA

Traditional CSA:

  • You fund the land and the seeds
  • The farmer cultivates and delivers the fruits to you
  • The seeds are free and reproducible

Digital CSA (G1FabLab):

  • You fund the servers and the open source code
  • The Captain maintains the services and makes them accessible to you
  • The code is free and reproducible: anyone can replant it

MULTIPASS: buy the fruits

As a MULTIPASS tenant, you buy the fruits of the garden:

  • 1 Zen/week: access to services (identity, uDRIVE, AI, N2 journal)
  • You consume without owning the land
  • If you stop paying, you no longer get the fruits

It is like buying vegetables at the market: convenient, but you do not own the field.

ZEN Card: own the land

As a ZEN Card cooperator, you co-own the garden:

  • +4 Zen/week: you fund the land (servers), the tools (software) and the seeds (R&D)
  • 128 GB NextCloud: your own plot in the garden
  • Voting rights: you decide what gets planted (1 person = 1 vote)
  • Edible forest: 1/3 of contributions buys real land and forests for the collective

It is like a CSA: you own the field, the tools and the seeds. The seeds are free: anyone can share them.

Sponsor: plant a tree

Sponsorship replaces the weekly fee with an annual contribution:

  • Satellite (50 euros/year): you plant a tree (share of a Raspberry Pi). NextCloud 128 GB + vote + no more weekly fees.
  • Constellation (540 euros/3 years): you plant a whole orchard (share of a GPU server). AI video access + priority services.

It is a donation to the collective, not a purchase. You fund the garden so that all gardeners benefit.

Shipowner and Captain

Shipowner = the landowner who lends their plot:

  • Hosts a server at home (fiber + electricity)
  • Receives 14 Zen/week hosting fee (the "field rent")

Captain = the farmer who cultivates the garden:

  • Installs, maintains and operates the station
  • Receives 28 Zen/week compensation (the "gardener's wage")

Shipowner and Captain can invoice in euros to G1FabLab via Open Collective.

The rule of thirds

Each contribution is automatically distributed by the code:

  • 1/3 Treasury: soil and water (electricity, bandwidth, replacement hardware)
  • 1/3 R&D: free reproducible seeds (open source code, new features)
  • 1/3 Commons: edible forest (acquisition of real land and forests for all cooperators)

Full transparency: all transactions are on the G1 blockchain, verifiable in real time.

Free seeds, shared garden

All the code is open source (AGPL-3.0).

Like free reproducible seeds: anyone can replant them, improve them, share them. The more gardeners there are, the more productive the garden.
