Day 4: Take action

You have tested the network for 3 days. Now, here is how to go from simple user to co-owner of the infrastructure.

3 levels of engagement. Choose the one that suits you.

Level 1: Sponsorship (50 euros/year)

You fund the purchase of hardware for the network:

  • 128 GB NextCloud: smartphone sync, files, contacts, calendar
  • Voting rights: 1 person = 1 vote on cooperative decisions
  • No more weekly fees: your annual contribution covers everything
  • Technical support: personalized assistance

Contribute via opencollective.com/monnaie-libre

Level 2: Shipowner (hosting)

You have fiber and space at home? Host an Astroport node:

  • Hardware provided by Sponsors (mini-PC or RPi5)
  • 14 Zen/week hosting fee
  • Simple contract: hosting agreement with G1FabLab
  • You provide: space, electricity, fiber, reboot if needed

The hardware remains property of the collective. Declare your income accordingly.

Level 3: Captain (operations)

You are technical? Operate and maintain stations:

  • 28 Zen/week compensation + surplus from your swarm
  • Missions: installation, maintenance, user onboarding, SSSS validation
  • Independence: self-employed, not salaried (business registration required)
  • Invoicing: via Open Collective, monthly bank transfer

Complete training and documentation provided.

The reimbursement service

Zen can be converted to euros via G1FabLab:

  • 1. The protocol measures your activity (hosting, maintenance)
  • 2. You issue an invoice in euros to G1FabLab
  • 3. The collective validates and triggers the bank transfer
  • 4. The corresponding Zen are settled

You only declare the euros received, not the Zen. The taxable event is the bank transfer.

Why this is different

  • No shareholders: G1FabLab is a cooperative (SCIC), not a startup.
  • No dividends: surpluses are reinvested in the common infrastructure.
  • Radical transparency: public blockchain accounting, verifiable in real time.
  • Open source code: the rules of the game are in the code (AGPL-3.0), auditable by everyone.

Analogy: Google = buying your vegetables at a supermarket. UPlanet = contributing to a shared garden whose harvest you share.
